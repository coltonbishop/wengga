Build a Translator
For Your Community

Wengga is a framework for the creation and collaborative
development of public translators for resource-scarce
languages. You provide the language data and Wengga
provides the computational resources and cutting-edge
neural machine translation techniques needed to build
a sophisticated translator for your language.

Provide a Tool

Preserve Culture

Help build something that will preserve your language, your words, and your culture for the future generations to come.

Bring People Together

Create a resource that will help others overcome language barriers and allow them to more easily connect with and understand your community.

Ongoing Language Projects

The neural translation architecture for the following langauges is already built! If you speak any of these langauges and would like to contribute to their development, click below!

Burarra

Burarra is an Australian Aboriginal language
spoken by about 1,000 people. The word 'Wengga'
is derived from Burarra and means 'language' or
'the sound of a person.'

Warlpiri

Warlpiri is an Australian Aboriginal language
spoken by about 3,000 people in Australia's
Northern Territory, making it one of the
most spoken aboriginal languages in Australia.

Australian Kriol

Australian Kriol is an English-based creole
language that developed from a pidgin used
in the early days of European colonization.
It is spoken by about 30,000 people.

Walmajarri

Walmajarri is an Australian Aboriginal language
spoken by about 300 people in Western
Australia.

Tiwi

Tiwi is an Australian Aboriginal language
spoken by over 2,000 people on the Tiwi islands.
It is one of about 10% of Australian languages
still being learned by children.

Iwaidja

Iwaidjar is an Australian aboriginal language
spoken by about 150 speakers in northern
Australia. It is also still being learnt by children.
